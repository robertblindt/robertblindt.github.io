About Me

My name is Robert Blindt and I am a new Software Engineer!

I respond to almost all permutations of my name, but prefer to go by Robert or Bobby. I went to the University of Hartford and graduated with a B.S. in Audio Engineering Technology. Unlike many widely advertised 'Audio Tech' programs like the one at 'Full Sail University' which predominantly focus on the art of mixing and recording, my program focused heavily on the electronic technology within the equipment and recording spaces to look wholistically at creating products or value in Pro Audio and Audio Product Development.

After graduating I started a job with Bose Corporation as an entry level Engineering Technician in their Consumer Electronics department fulfilling requests from engineers to test and modify development and production product. My strong fundamentals in electronics and acoustics quickly allowed me to become an embedded team member on many projects, and learn and apply all sorts of electro-acoustic and electro-mechanical principals in test and development. During a restructuring I [moved to/stayed with] the Aviation and Military Department and began liaising more closely with engineers and mechanical designers. Unfortunately restructuring happened once again a couple years later as a result of chip shortages and I was made redundant, but it was a blessing in disguise. Aspects of the job precluded me from having a real work-life-balance and getting to the bottom of some medical issues. Since then I have regained control of my health, and understand how to manage my body!

I love to tinker and improve the things around me. I have built, modified, and designed keyboards, amplifies, fixtures, tools, cases, housings, darts, darting accessories, and more. I love practicing and watching Darts. I mostly keep up with the PDC, but will also put on the CDC, ADO, or even a local streamed event if I happen to see it!

Recently I took a Data Analytics course with a local institution called Network Technology Academy Institute, but the outputs were not enough to make myself totally saleable outside of the types of roles I previously served. I did a couple projects using Python and SQL that can be found in my Github (on the right). I already have my PCEP, but I am looking to cement my Python fundamentals, and gain the skills to become a full stack developer.

Take a Look at my Darts Designs

I designed some custom darts for myself and a friend!

When I first started learning to play darts I could barely hit the board. Within a month I got used to the basics of throwing a dart but something didn't feel quite right. I watched a few videos about darts set-ups and design, and quickly bought a few different sets to test out what I liked. What I found was that I liked a front weighted, short dart, with not much grip and a slightly longer point. I also noticed that my finger wanted to drape onto the point, and with the way my darts fly, I frequently had lip-to-flight 'bounce outs'. For my darts, I started using the Jelle Klaasen Phase 1 darts, but wanted to have a slightly more bulbus front end, a shallower but wider reference point, and a less extreme drop off on the back end. I was luck enough to have access to a high quality SLA printer so I had the opportunity hold the design prior to sending it off to get machined, but none the less the modifications I made impacted my grip the way I expected it to.

I got a bunch of my friends into darts during the COVID lockdowns, and one of them really took a liking to it! He now plays in lots of drop-ins and local leagues, and has started really practicing. He always complained about a discomfort with his darts, but couldn't put his finger on what it was. I brought over a bunch of my darts for him to try out, and he actually went and bought a set of the Jelle Klaasens as well! Some months passed and he was flip flopping between two sets, the Klaasens and Joe Cullens. After watching him throw a little bit with both sets, it became apparent that with the Cullens he was more naturally grasping the dart, while with the Klaasens he was almost forcing a pinch grip at the center point. He tried out my custom darts, and he once again grasped the dart more naturally but he didn't like how thin the dart became where his thumb rested. So for his darts, I once again started with the the Jelle Klaasens dart, but aimed to make a similar front end to my dart, but maintain the center weighting and add the central grip from the Cullens to the grip space.

Some other 3D Printables

In my previous role I fell in love with the power of 3D printing, and have been luck enough to still be able to borrow time on some friends printers!

I have designed and printed all sorts of random stuff to help people with their hobbies. One of the designs I am most proud of is a system of adaptors and plates that allowed a 'tinywhoop' sized FPV drone to carry an HD camera system with an external gyro unit for image stabilization. Many of my measurements had to be within a millimeter to be successful, and it all worked out!

I have also designed some less impressive looking items like a jig to modify a darts flight to mimic a flight that is no longer or never was in production, cheap lighting solutions for dartboards, and all sorts of cases and boxes including the case for the keyboard I use every day!
